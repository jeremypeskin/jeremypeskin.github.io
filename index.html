Q: Is a work visa required for Canadians visiting their Company's US office?

Our Canadian company has a US office in New York City. Are Canadian employees of our allowed to visit these offices as part of a business trip without obtaining a US work visa?

James E. Pittman, Esq.
Contact James

Answered:
Feb 11, 2016

A:

Yes. Canadians, and citizens of other countries as well, can visit their company's US offices and engage in activities which are consistent with being a business visitor without the need for a work visa. Activities which are allowed include attending meetings, visiting facilities, participating in short term trainings, giving a presentation, receiving an award, and the like. Moreover, Canadians can actually be admitted as a business visitor right at the US border without first getting a visa from a US consulate. The key thing here is that someone coming as a business visitor cannot be labor for hire – so they can not be put on a US company or affiliate's payroll while in that status.
